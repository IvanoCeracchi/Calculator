Merchant Fee Comparison

Enter the figures from your latest merchant statement, add the rates from the offer you're comparing, and press Calculate to see the difference side by side.

Where do I find these numbers? (open me first)

For each card type you'll need four figures: net sales, the total MSF (merchant service fee) charged, and the interchange and scheme portions of that fee. Every provider shows these slightly differently, but the same four numbers are always there. Two common formats below — find the one closer to your statement.

Format A — statement breaks down interchange, scheme and acquiring

Your statement lists Net Sales, Interchange, Scheme and Margin as separate columns for each card type.

Net sales$101,512.63 Interchange$537.66 Scheme$121.82 Margin$245.96 Total MSF$905.44

Copy Net sales straight across. Add Interchange + Scheme + Margin to get Total MSF (or copy the total if your statement already shows one).

Format B — statement breaks down interchange, scheme and acquiring + intl

Your statement writes the fee out as a running total per card type, with each component named and its rate shown alongside it, rather than as separate columns.

Net sales$50,000.00 Interchange$250.00 Scheme$60.00 Acquiring + Intl fee$120.00 Total MSF$430.00

Take the interchange and scheme amounts as named. Add together every other fee on that line (acquiring, margin, international loading, or similar) into one combined figure. Interchange + Scheme + that combined figure is your Total MSF.

Visa Domestic = Visa debit + credit (local) Mastercard Domestic = Mastercard debit + credit (local) Visa / Mastercard International = overseas-issued cards

This calculator covers Mastercard, Visa, DCC and EFTPOS only. Other payment types — Discover, UnionPay, Amex — are not included in the calculation.
